Privacy Policy

Trace Nook

Last updated: 2026-07-19

App: Trace Nook
Developer: sens9012

Summary

Trace Nook is a local-first, offline home inventory app that remembers where your household items are and when they expire. It has no account system, no ads, and no cloud sync. Trace Nook does not collect, upload, or share any of your personal data. Everything you create stays on your own device.

Data Collection

Trace Nook does not collect personal data from your device and does not send it anywhere. There is no user account, no sign-in, no analytics account, and no advertising SDK that transmits your information off the device.

Data Stored Locally on Your Device

The following is created and stored only on your device, and is never transmitted to us or to any third-party server:

  • Items you record (names, notes, categories, quantities, units)
  • Photos attached to items
  • Storage locations (rooms, containers, compartments)
  • Expiry dates and low-stock thresholds
  • Barcodes you scan and the local "barcode learning" data derived from them
  • QR labels you generate for boxes and drawers
  • Search history and frequently-used searches (learned locally)
  • App settings (language, reminders)
  • Backup files you export

This data lives in Trace Nook's local storage on your device. If you uninstall the app or clear its data, this information is removed from the device (except for backup files you have already exported elsewhere).

Camera

Trace Nook uses the camera, only when you actively invoke it, for three purposes: taking photos of items, scanning product barcodes, and scanning the QR labels you created. Camera images are processed on the device. No image or scan result is ever uploaded.

Photos

When you attach a photo to an item, Trace Nook saves an internal copy inside the app. Deleting an item in Trace Nook only removes Trace Nook's internal copy — it does not delete the original photo in your phone's photo gallery. Photos are never uploaded to any server.

On-Device Text Recognition (OCR)

Trace Nook can optionally recognize text on packaging (for example, to help name an item or read an expiry date). This recognition runs entirely on your device using an on-device machine-learning library. The images and recognized text are not sent to any server.

Family Sync over Local Network (Optional)

Trace Nook offers an optional family sync feature that shares your inventory between your own devices / family members' devices. This sync is peer-to-peer within your local Wi-Fi network and is end-to-end encrypted. It does not pass through our servers or any third-party relay — no such servers exist. The feature is off by default and only exchanges data with devices you explicitly pair.

Backups

Backups are exported manually by you as encrypted files (.tnbackup). Trace Nook does not store your backup password, and it is never sent anywhere. If you forget the backup password, the backup cannot be recovered. You are responsible for keeping your exported backup files and their passwords safe.

Notifications

Trace Nook uses local notifications scheduled on your device to remind you about expired, expiring, or low-stock items. We do not use a remote push notification service to send reminders.

Purchases

Trace Nook's core features are free. It may offer optional one-time paid upgrades. Purchases are processed by Google Play. Trace Nook does not receive or store your payment card, bank account, or any payment credentials. The app may store a local unlock status on your device, and may check your purchase status through Google Play in order to restore access.

Children's Privacy

Trace Nook is not directed to children. Since Trace Nook does not collect personal data from users, we do not knowingly collect personal data from children.

Changes to This Policy
